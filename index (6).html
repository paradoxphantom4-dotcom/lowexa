Ch. 4 — Everywhere You Did Not Expect

Your Morning Tea Bag

11.6 billion
particles

Released into a single cup of tea by a nylon or polypropylene tea bag steeped at 95°C. If you drink two a day, you are consuming roughly 23 billion microplastic particles from tea alone.

Hernandez et al., Environmental Science & Technology, 2019
Ch. 4 — Everywhere You Did Not Expect

Your Paper Coffee Cup

25,000
particles

Released into 100ml of hot liquid by the polyethylene lining inside a standard paper cup, within 15 minutes. The lining is invisible to most people.

Bhatt et al., Journal of Hazardous Materials, 2020
Ch. 4 — Everywhere You Did Not Expect

Your Baby's Bottle

Up to 16.2M
per litre

Released during standard sterilisation and formula preparation at 70°C. Published in Nature Food by Trinity College Dublin. Glass bottles produced zero detectable particles.

Li et al., Nature Food, 2020
